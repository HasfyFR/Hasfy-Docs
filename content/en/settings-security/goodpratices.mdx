# Security best practices

This guide gathers security recommendations to protect your organization and your data on Hasfy. Following these best practices significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access and compromise.

## Passwords

### Rules enforced by Hasfy

Hasfy enforces a password length of **8 to 128 characters**. No complexity rule (uppercase, digit, special character) is enforced by the application.

### Our recommendations

| Practice | Recommendation |
|----------|---------------|
| Length | 12 characters or more |
| Complexity | Mix letters, digits, and special characters |
| Uniqueness | Never reuse the same password as on other services |
| Manager | Use a password manager (1Password, Bitwarden, etc.) to generate and store your passwords |
| Change | Change your password immediately if you suspect a compromise |
| Sharing | Never share your password, even with a colleague or the Hasfy team |

### Passphrases

An effective technique is to use a **passphrase**: a string of random words that's easy to remember but hard to guess. For example, `horse-battery-correct-stapler` is longer, more memorable, and more secure than `P@ssw0rd123!`.

## Enable 2FA on every account

Enabling 2FA is the single most impactful security measure you can take. It's available to **all roles** (Owner, Admin, Technician, Employee). Each user enables it individually from **Settings &gt; Security**. See the [Two-factor authentication](/settings-security/mfas) guide.

> **Recommendation**: enable 2FA on every account in your organization, prioritizing **Owner**, **Admin**, and **Technician** accounts, which have access to your fleet's data and equipment.

## Sessions

Hasfy automatically applies the following rules to your sessions, with no configuration needed:

- **Inactivity timeout**: a session expires after 24 hours without activity
- **Maximum lifetime**: a session expires after 7 days at the latest, even if it remains active in the meantime
- **Automatic sign-out of other sessions**: on every successful login, Hasfy automatically signs you out of all your other active sessions on your other devices

> **Recommendation**: on a shared computer, always sign out after use: don't rely solely on automatic expiration.

### Password reset

The link you receive via **"Forgot password?"** has a limited validity period. If it has expired, simply request a new one.

### Protection against repeated login attempts

Our authentication provider (Supabase Auth) includes basic protections against brute-force attacks on the login page.

## Browser security

### Recommendations

- Use an **up-to-date browser**: the latest version of Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge
- **HTTPS**: make sure the URL shows `https://app.hasfy.fr` with the padlock
- **Extensions**: limit browser extensions, as some can intercept your data
- **Private mode**: use private browsing mode on shared computers
- **Autofill**: if you don't use a password manager, disable automatic password saving in your browser

### Phishing detection

Hasfy will never ask you for:
- Your password by email or phone
- To click a link to "verify" your account (except as part of actions you've just initiated)
- To install software outside the official agent installation process

If you receive a suspicious email claiming to be from Hasfy, forward it to us via the [Contact](/contactus) page.

## API keys

If you use the Hasfy API (see [API documentation](/api)), follow these principles:

| Practice | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Principle of least privilege** | Create keys with only the scopes they need |
| **Expiration** | At creation, choose a validity period: never, 30, 90, 180, or 365 days |
| **Regular rotation** | To enforce periodic rotation, choose a 90-day expiration at creation rather than "never" |
| **Separation** | Use a different key for each application or integration |
| **Secure storage** | Never hard-code a key in source code: use environment variables or a secrets vault |
| **Immediate revocation** | Revoke a key as soon as it's no longer needed or if you have any doubt |

### Monitoring

In **Settings &gt; API**, each key directly displays its **last use** (date and IP address) and its **call count**, with no separate tab needed. Key creation and revocation are recorded in the audit log (**Settings &gt; Audit**).



## Access control and offboarding

### Principle of least privilege

Assign each user the **minimum role necessary** to perform their tasks. See the full permissions breakdown by role on the [Users & Groups](/core-features/usermanagement) page:

- A user who only creates tickets → **Employee**
- A technician who manages equipment and tickets → **Technician**
- An IT manager who configures the organization → **Admin**
- The person responsible for billing and the organization → **Owner**

### Regular access reviews

Carry out access reviews regularly from the [Users & Groups](/core-features/usermanagement) page:

1. Check that **roles** still match each member's current responsibilities
2. **Disable** the accounts of people who have left the organization
3. **Adjust** roles if responsibilities have changed

### Offboarding checklist

When a team member leaves your organization, from the [Users & Groups](/core-features/usermanagement) page (Admin/Owner):

- [ ] Disable their account, or remove them from the organization
- [ ] If their responsibilities are taken over by someone else, adjust that person's role
- [ ] Revoke their personal API keys (an Admin/Owner can revoke any member's keys)
- [ ] Check the audit log (**Settings &gt; Audit**) for their most recent actions

## Agent and equipment enrollment security

The Hasfy agent installed on your machines communicates with Hasfy servers over HTTPS.

### Install tokens

To install the agent on a piece of equipment, you generate an **install token** from the **Equipment &gt; Install** page:

- **Single-use**: the token is invalidated as soon as it's used once
- **Limited lifetime**: 30 minutes for an interactive install from the app, 7 days for an email invitation or a bulk (MDM) deployment
- **Scoped to your organization**: a token can only be used for your organization
- **Manual revocation**: you can revoke a token at any time from the same page

Every token generation, use, and revocation is recorded in the audit log (**Settings &gt; Audit**).

## Reporting a security incident

If you suspect a security incident:

1. **Change your password immediately**
2. If 2FA isn't enabled yet, enable it (see [Two-factor authentication](/settings-security/mfas)): your next login will automatically sign out your other sessions
3. Contact our team via the [Contact](/contactus) page, describing the incident
4. If applicable, notify your organization's Owner
