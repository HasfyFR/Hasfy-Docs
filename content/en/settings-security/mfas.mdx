# Multi-factor authentication (MFA)

Multi-factor authentication (MFA), also known as two-factor authentication (2FA), adds an **extra layer of security** to your Hasfy account. In addition to your password, you must provide a second identification factor to log in.

## Why enable MFA?

A password alone is vulnerable: it can be guessed, obtained through phishing, or exposed in a data breach. MFA protects your account even if your password is compromised, because the attacker would also need your second factor.

**Risks without MFA:**
- Unauthorized access in the event of a password leak
- Vulnerability to phishing and social engineering
- Potential impact on the entire organization if an administrator account is compromised

> **Strong recommendation**: Hasfy recommends enabling MFA for **all accounts**, and especially for **Owner** and **Admin** roles. The Owner can also make MFA mandatory for all members of the organization.

## Supported authentication methods

### Authenticator app (TOTP)

The recommended method uses an **authenticator app** that generates temporary codes (TOTP — Time-based One-Time Password):

**Compatible apps:**
- Google Authenticator (iOS, Android)
- Microsoft Authenticator (iOS, Android)
- Authy (iOS, Android, Desktop)
- 1Password, Bitwarden, and other password managers that support TOTP

**How it works:**
1. You scan a QR code with your authenticator app
2. The app generates a 6-digit code that changes every 30 seconds
3. At each login, after your password, you enter the code shown in the app

### Email verification

As an alternative to a TOTP app, you can receive a **verification code by email**:

- A 6-digit code is sent to your account email address
- The code is valid for 10 minutes
- This method is less secure than TOTP (it depends on the security of your email)

> **Note**: Email verification is considered a less secure second factor. If your mailbox is compromised, this method no longer protects your account. **Prefer the TOTP app.**

## Enabling MFA

### Enabling with a TOTP app

1. Go to **Settings &gt; My account &gt; Security &gt; Two-factor authentication**
2. Click **"Enable 2FA"**
3. Enter your **password** to confirm your identity
4. Choose the **"Authenticator app"** method
5. A **QR code** is displayed on screen
6. Open your authenticator app and scan the QR code
   - *Or* manually enter the **secret key** shown below the QR code
7. The app adds a "Hasfy" entry and starts generating codes
8. Enter the **6-digit code** currently shown in the app to verify the setup
9. MFA is enabled — **recovery codes are generated**

### Enabling with email verification

1. Follow steps 1 to 4 above
2. Choose the **"Email verification"** method
3. A verification code is sent to your email address
4. Enter the code you received
5. Email-based MFA is enabled

## Recovery codes

When you enable MFA, Hasfy generates **10 single-use recovery codes**. These codes let you log in if you lose access to your authenticator app.

### Managing your codes

- **Download**: click "Download codes" to save a text file
- **Copy**: copy the codes into a password manager
- **Print**: print the codes and store them somewhere safe

> **Very important**: Keep your recovery codes in a **safe place separate** from your main device. If you lose both your authenticator app and your recovery codes, you risk losing access to your account.

### Using a recovery code

1. On the login page, enter your email and password
2. At the MFA step, click **"Use a recovery code"**
3. Enter one of your recovery codes
4. You're logged in — **the used code is invalidated**

### Regenerating codes

If you've used several codes or suspect a compromise:

1. Go to **Settings &gt; My account &gt; Security &gt; 2FA**
2. Click **"Regenerate recovery codes"**
3. Confirm with your password
4. The old codes are invalidated and 10 new codes are generated

## Disabling MFA

To disable two-factor authentication:

1. Go to **Settings &gt; My account &gt; Security &gt; 2FA**
2. Click **"Disable 2FA"**
3. Enter your **password** and a **TOTP code** (or a recovery code)
4. Confirm the deactivation

> **Warning**: Disabling MFA significantly reduces the security of your account. Only disable it temporarily (e.g., when changing phones) and re-enable it as soon as possible.

## Mandatory MFA

The Owner can make MFA mandatory for all members of the organization:

1. Go to **Settings &gt; Organization &gt; Security**
2. Enable **"Mandatory MFA"**
3. Set the **grace period**: the number of days users have to enable MFA
4. Users without MFA receive a notification and are redirected to setup at every login
5. After the grace period, accounts without MFA can no longer log in

## Best practices

| Practice | Recommendation |
|----------|---------------|
| Enable MFA for all accounts | ✅ Strongly recommended |
| Use a TOTP app rather than email | ✅ More secure |
| Back up your recovery codes | ✅ Essential |
| Use a password manager that supports TOTP | ✅ Convenient and secure |
| Disable MFA | ❌ Avoid unless necessary |
| Share your recovery codes | ❌ Never do this |
| Store codes on the same device as the app | ❌ Risk of losing both at once |
