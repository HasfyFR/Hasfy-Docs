# Two-factor authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication (2FA), also known as multi-factor authentication (MFA), adds an **extra layer of security** to your Hasfy account. In addition to your password, you must provide a second factor (a one-time code generated by an app) to sign in.

## Why enable 2FA?

A password alone is vulnerable: it can be guessed, obtained through phishing, or exposed in a data breach. 2FA protects your account even if your password is compromised, since access to your authenticator app would also be required.

> **Strong recommendation**: enable 2FA on your account, especially if you hold an **Owner**, **Admin**, or **Technician** role. These accounts have access to your organization's data and equipment, and their compromise would have a much wider impact than a standard account.

## How to enable it

Hasfy supports 2FA via the **TOTP** standard (Time-based One-Time Password), with an authenticator app such as Google Authenticator, Authy, or 1Password.

1. Open the **Settings** window, **Security** section
2. Click **"Enable 2FA"**
3. A **QR code** and a **secret** (with a copy button) are displayed
4. Scan the QR code with your authenticator app (or enter the secret manually)
5. The app generates a **6-digit code**, refreshed every 30 seconds
6. Enter this code in Hasfy to verify and enable 2FA

Once enabled, the date the factor was added is displayed ("Added on ..."). 2FA is available to **all roles** (Owner, Admin, Technician, Employee): each user enables and manages their own 2FA, independently of other organization members.

## Compatible apps

Hasfy relies on the **TOTP** standard, compatible with most authenticator apps: Google Authenticator, Authy, 1Password, Microsoft Authenticator, etc.

> Hasfy does not offer SMS or email-based 2FA, nor recovery (backup) codes. If you lose access to your authenticator app, contact our team via the [Contact](/contactus) page.

## How to disable it

1. Open **Settings &gt; Security**
2. Click **"Remove"** on the active factor
3. Confirm the deactivation

> **Warning**: disabling 2FA reduces your account's security. Only disable it temporarily (for example when changing phones) and re-enable it as soon as possible.



## Session security

- On every login, if an active 2FA factor exists on your account, Hasfy asks for your TOTP code before establishing the session
- Once 2FA is validated (or if it isn't enabled), Hasfy automatically signs you out of all your **other active sessions** on your other devices

> This isn't a "manage my devices" feature: it's an automatic security behavior applied on every login, with no dedicated session-management interface.

## Traceability

Every activation and deactivation of 2FA on an account is recorded in the organization's **audit log** ("MFA enabled" / "MFA disabled" entries), visible to **Owner** and **Admin** roles from **Settings &gt; Audit**, with CSV/JSON/Markdown export and filters by severity and date range.
