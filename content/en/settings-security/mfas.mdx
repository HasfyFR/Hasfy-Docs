# Two-factor authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds a second layer of security to your Hasfy account: in addition to your password, you'll need to enter a temporary code generated by an authenticator app to log in.

## Why enable 2FA?

Enabling 2FA significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access to your account, even if your password is compromised (phishing, leak, reuse on another site).

It is especially **recommended for administrator and technician accounts**, which have access to your organization's client data, equipment inventory, and tickets.

## How to enable it

Hasfy uses **TOTP** (Time-based One-Time Password) authentication via an authenticator app — there is no email-based or SMS-based method.

1. Go to **Settings &gt; My Account &gt; Security**
2. Click **"Enable two-factor authentication"**
3. Scan the **QR code** displayed using an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, etc.)
4. Enter the **6-digit validation code** generated by the app to confirm activation

Once enabled, a code will be requested in addition to your password each time you log in.

## How to disable it

1. Go to **Settings &gt; My Account &gt; Security**
2. Click **"Disable two-factor authentication"**
3. Confirm the deactivation

> **Note**: disabling 2FA reduces the security of your account. We recommend keeping it enabled, especially for accounts with administrator or technician privileges.

## Traceability

Every activation or deactivation of two-factor authentication is recorded in your organization's **audit log** ("MFA enabled" / "MFA disabled" entries), visible to administrators via **Settings &gt; Security &gt; Audit log**.
