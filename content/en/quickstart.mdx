# Quick Start

This guide walks you through everything from creating your account to viewing your first monitoring metrics. Plan for about **10 minutes** to complete all the steps.

## Prerequisites

Before you begin, make sure you have:

- A valid professional email address
- A modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
- A workstation (Windows 10+, macOS 12+, or Linux) for installing the agent *(optional for this initial setup)*

## Step 1: Create your account

1. Go to **[app.hasfy.fr](https://app.hasfy.fr)** and click **"Create an account"**
2. Enter your **email address** and choose a **secure password** (minimum 8 characters, including an uppercase letter, a lowercase letter, and a digit)
3. You can also sign up via a **magic link** sent by email — no password required in this case
4. Confirm your registration by clicking the confirmation link you receive by email
5. You'll be redirected to the onboarding screen

> **Tip**: The magic link is especially handy for a quick first login. You can set up a permanent password later in your account settings.

## Step 2: Complete onboarding

Hasfy's onboarding guides you through the initial setup of your workspace:

1. **Personal information**: fill in your first name, last name, and contact phone number
2. **Creating your organization**: give your organization a name (e.g. "Acme Corp IT") and specify its industry
3. **Calibration questions**: answer a few questions about the size of your IT fleet, your top priorities, and your level of experience. These answers allow Hasfy to personalize your experience
4. **Profile picture** *(optional)*: upload an avatar to personalize your account
5. Click **"Finish setup"**

You'll then land on your organization's **dashboard**, ready to be populated.

## Step 3: Add your first piece of equipment

From the dashboard, go to the **Equipment** module:

1. Click the **"+ Add equipment"** button at the top right
2. Fill in the basic information:
   - **Name**: a meaningful identifier (e.g. "PC-Office-Martin", "Server-Prod-01")
   - **Type**: select the equipment type (Workstation, Server, Printer, Network, Mobile, Other)
   - **Operating system**: Windows, macOS, Linux, or Other
   - **Serial number** *(optional)*: the manufacturer's serial number
   - **Assignment** *(optional)*: assign the equipment to a user in your organization
3. Add **tags** to make filtering easier (e.g. "Accounting", "Server room", "Critical")
4. Click **"Save"**

The equipment now appears in your inventory with the status **"Active — Awaiting agent"**.

> **Note**: You can add equipment manually even without installing the agent. The inventory will then work as a static registry. The agent adds the dimension of real-time monitoring.

## Step 4: Install the Hasfy agent

The Hasfy agent is lightweight software that installs on every machine you want to monitor. It automatically reports system metrics to your dashboard.

### Download

1. From the equipment's record, click **"Install agent"**, or go to **Settings &gt; Agents &gt; Download**
2. Select the **operating system** of the target machine:
   - **Windows**: `.exe` installer (compatible with Windows 10 and later)
   - **macOS**: `.dmg` installer (compatible with macOS 12 Monterey and later)
   - **Linux**: `.deb` package (Debian/Ubuntu) or `.rpm` package (RHEL/Fedora)

### Installation

**On Windows:**
1. Run the downloaded `.exe` file
2. Follow the installation wizard (accept the terms, choose the installation folder)
3. At the "Configuration" step, paste the **enrollment key** provided by Hasfy
4. The agent starts automatically as a Windows service

**On macOS:**
1. Open the `.dmg` file and drag the application into the Applications folder
2. Launch the application — it will ask for the **enrollment key**
3. Grant the requested system permissions (disk access, network)
4. The agent runs in the background and starts automatically on boot

**On Linux:**
```bash
# Debian/Ubuntu
sudo dpkg -i hasfy-agent_latest_amd64.deb
sudo hasfy-agent enroll --key YOUR_ENROLLMENT_KEY
sudo systemctl enable --now hasfy-agent

# RHEL/Fedora
sudo rpm -i hasfy-agent_latest_x86_64.rpm
sudo hasfy-agent enroll --key YOUR_ENROLLMENT_KEY
sudo systemctl enable --now hasfy-agent
```

### Verification

After installation, go back to Hasfy:
1. Open the relevant equipment's record
2. The status changes from **"Awaiting agent"** to **"Agent connected"** (green indicator)
3. The first metrics appear within seconds: CPU, RAM, disk space, network

## Step 5: Explore the dashboard

Go to the **Dashboard** page to view your data:

- **Summary cards**: total number of equipment, equipment online, active alerts, open tickets
- **Health chart**: overall fleet status (normal, warning, critical)
- **Real-time metrics**: CPU, RAM, and disk usage of your monitored machines
- **Recent activity**: the latest actions performed in your organization
