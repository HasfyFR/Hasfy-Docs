# Notifications

Hasfy's notification system keeps you informed in real time about the activity of your IT fleet and your team's actions. Notifications are designed to be relevant and unobtrusive, with granular control over what you receive.

## Notification center

The **notification center** is accessible from the 🔔 icon in the navigation bar. A numeric badge shows the number of unread notifications.

### Center interface

Clicking the icon opens a side panel containing:

- **Chronological list**: all your notifications, from most recent to oldest
- **Quick filters**: All, Unread, Critical
- **Bulk actions**: "Mark all as read", "Clear read notifications"

Each notification displays:
- **Icon**: visually identifies the type (ticket, equipment, system, calendar)
- **Title**: a summary of the event
- **Detail**: additional information (equipment name, ticket number, etc.)
- **Timestamp**: date and time of the event
- **Link**: click to go directly to the relevant item

## Notification types

| Event | Criticality | Description |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Agent disconnected | 🔴 Critical | A monitoring agent has not communicated for more than 5 minutes |
| Agent reconnected | 🟢 Info | A previously disconnected agent is back online |

## Notification channels

### In-app

In-app notifications are the main channel. They appear in the notification center and are always enabled. They cannot be completely disabled, but they can be filtered by category.

### Email

Email notifications send a message to your account's email address for important events. By default, only **critical** and **warning** notifications are sent by email.

Sending frequency configuration:

- **Real time**: one email per notification
- **Hourly digest**: one summary email per hour
- **Daily digest**: one summary email per day
- **Disabled**: no email (in-app only)

To configure: **Settings &gt; Preferences &gt; Notifications &gt; Email**.

## Preference configuration

### By category

Go to **Settings &gt; Preferences &gt; Notifications** to fine-tune your notifications:

For each category (Equipment, Tickets, Calendar, Organization, System), independently configure:

1. **In-app**: enabled/disabled per event type
2. **Email**: enabled/disabled per event type
3. **Minimum criticality**: only receive notifications at or above a minimum level (e.g. "Warning and above")

### Monitoring alert thresholds

Customize the thresholds that trigger monitoring alerts:

| Metric | Warning threshold (default) | Critical threshold (default) |
|----------|------|---------|
| CPU | 80% | 95% |
| RAM | 85% | 95% |
| Disk | 80% | 90% |
| Agent offline | 5 min | 15 min |

To change these thresholds: **Settings &gt; Alerts &gt; Monitoring**.

### Quiet hours

Enable quiet hours to receive only **critical** notifications during certain time ranges:

1. **Settings &gt; Preferences &gt; Notifications &gt; Quiet hours**
2. Set the time range (e.g. 8 PM - 8 AM)
3. Select the relevant days
4. Non-critical notifications will be queued and delivered at the end of the quiet period

## Managing notifications

### Marking as read/unread

- **Individually**: click the button to the right of each notification
- **In bulk**: use "Mark all as read" at the top of the notification center

### Deleting notifications

Notifications are automatically deleted after **30 days**. You can delete them manually using the "Clear read notifications" button.

### Follow/Unfollow

For tickets, you can choose to **follow** or **unfollow** a specific ticket:
- **Automatic following**: you automatically follow tickets you create or are assigned to
- **Manual following**: click the 🔔 icon on a ticket to follow or unfollow it
