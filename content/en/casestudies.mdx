## Case 1: MédiSanté — Group of clinics (Healthcare)

### Background

**MédiSanté** is a group of 4 private clinics in the Paris region (Île-de-France) employing 280 people. Their IT fleet includes 150 workstations, 12 servers, 35 printers, and numerous network devices spread across 4 geographic sites.

**IT team**: 1 IT manager and 2 technicians.

### Challenges before Hasfy

- **Spreadsheet-based inventory**: a shared Excel file served as the inventory, often outdated and containing data entry errors
- **Email-based requests**: support requests came in by email, phone, or in person, with no centralized tracking. Requests were regularly lost or handled twice
- **No monitoring**: server failures were detected only when users reported a malfunction, sometimes hours after the problem began
- **Healthcare compliance**: the medical sector imposes strict requirements regarding traceability and system availability
- **Frequent travel**: technicians spent a lot of time traveling between the 4 sites with no visibility into priorities

### Solution implemented

1. **Inventory migration**: CSV import of the entire fleet from the existing spreadsheet into Hasfy
2. **Agent deployment**: installation of the Hasfy agent on the 150 workstations and 12 servers via GPO (Group Policy Object)
3. **Centralized ticketing**: training the 280 employees to create tickets via the simplified Employee interface
4. **Monitoring alerts**: configuration of critical alerts on servers (disk space &lt; 15%, CPU &gt; 90% for 5 min, agent offline)
5. **Maintenance calendar**: scheduling of recurring monthly maintenance windows for each site

### Results after 6 months

| Metric | Before Hasfy | After Hasfy | Change |
|------------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| Time to detect a server failure | 2-4 hours | &lt; 5 minutes | -97% |
| Lost / unhandled tickets | ~15/month | 0 | -100% |
| Average resolution time | Not measured | 4.2 hours | Now measurable |
| SLA compliance rate | Not measured | 91% | Now measurable |
| Time spent managing inventory | 8 h/month | 1 h/month | -87% |
| Unnecessary trips (remote diagnosis) | ~20/month | 5/month | -75% |

> *"Hasfy allowed us to move from reactive to proactive management of our fleet. The monitoring alerts detected an overloaded patient records server one night — we were able to step in before the clinic opened. Before, we would have found out from doctors' complaints the next morning."*
> — IT Manager, MédiSanté

## Case 2: TechServ Solutions — MSP provider (IT Services)

### Background

**TechServ Solutions** is a managed IT services provider (MSP) based in Lyon, managing the fleets of **12 SMB clients** representing a total of 450 pieces of equipment. The team consists of 6 technicians and 1 technical director.

### Challenges before Hasfy

- **Fragmented tools**: each client had its own system (some used free tools, others spreadsheets, others nothing at all)
- **No multi-client visibility**: impossible to see the overall status of all fleets at a glance
- **Time-consuming reporting**: producing monthly reports for each client required hours of manual compilation
- **Billing difficulties**: time spent on each client wasn't precisely tracked, making flat-rate billing unprofitable
- **Technician turnover**: when a technician left the team, their knowledge of client fleets left with them

### Solution implemented

1. **One organization per client**: creation of a dedicated Hasfy organization for each of the 12 clients, with strict data separation
2. **Differentiated roles**: clients access their space as Employees (creating tickets, viewing their equipment), technicians as Technicians
3. **Agents deployed systematically**: installation of the agent across all 450 monitored machines
4. **Automatic reports**: configuration of weekly reports automatically sent to each client
5. **Time tracking**: use of ticketing to track time spent on each intervention

### Results after 1 year

| Metric | Before Hasfy | After Hasfy | Change |
|------------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| Time spent producing reports | 16 h/month | 0 (automated) | -100% |
| Customer satisfaction (NPS) | 32 | 68 | +113% |
| Average resolution time | Not tracked | 3.1 hours | Now measurable |
| Preventive interventions (vs. reactive) | 10% | 45% | +350% |
| Client churn | 2 clients/year | 0 | -100% |
| New technician onboarding | 2 weeks | 3 days | -80% |

> *"As an MSP, multi-client visibility is critical. With Hasfy, I can see at a glance which fleets have alerts, which tickets are overdue, and where to focus my resources. The automatic reports have transformed our client relationships — they receive a professional summary every week without us having to do anything."*
> — Technical Director, TechServ Solutions

## Case 3: Atelier Dubois — Industrial SMB (Manufacturing)

### Background

**Atelier Dubois** is an industrial SMB with 45 employees specializing in precision machining, based in Normandy. Its IT fleet is modest: 30 workstations, 2 servers, 8 printers, and 5 network-connected CNC machine tools.

**IT team**: none — IT management is handled by the office manager, with occasional support from a local provider.

### Challenges before Hasfy

- **No management tool**: the entire fleet existed "in the head" of the office manager and on a few sticky notes
- **Reliance on the provider**: every problem required a call to the provider, with no history of past interventions
- **Production stoppages**: a failure on a workstation connected to a CNC machine tool would halt production, at an estimated cost of €500/hour
- **Limited budget**: no budget for a traditional ITSM tool costing tens of thousands of euros per year
- **No IT skills**: the office manager had no IT training

### Solution implemented

1. **Hasfy Pro subscription**: signed up with **a single seat** (the office manager, as administrator) at €150/month under the Early Access rate — the 7 other accounts (workshop managers and management) are standard users, included for free in the 250-account quota unlocked by that seat
2. **Complete inventory**: creation of records for the 45 pieces of equipment with tags by workshop and criticality
3. **Agents on critical machines**: installation of the agent on the 5 workstations connected to the machine tools and the 2 servers
4. **Critical alerts**: configuration of immediate alerts (email + in-app) for any agent disconnection on production workstations
5. **Marketplace**: use of the marketplace to find a certified technician for migrating an aging server

### Results after 4 months

| Metric | Before Hasfy | After Hasfy | Change |
|------------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| IT-related production downtime | ~6 h/month | &lt; 1 h/month | -83% |
| Cost of production downtime | ~€3,000/month | ~€500/month | -83% |
| Cost of the tool | 0 (but high losses) | €150/month | ROI within the first week |
| Knowledge of the fleet | None (sticky notes) | Complete and up to date | Major |
| Independence from provider | Total reliance | 70% of requests handled in-house | +70% |

> *"I'm not an IT person, and that's exactly why Hasfy works for me. The interface is clear, I can immediately see if a production workstation has a problem, and I can create a ticket for our provider in 30 seconds instead of spending 20 minutes on the phone explaining the situation. For €150 a month and a single seat, it's the most profitable investment we've made this year."*
> — Office Manager, Atelier Dubois
