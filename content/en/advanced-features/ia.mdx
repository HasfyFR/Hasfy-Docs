# AI Assistant

Hasfy's **AI Assistant** leverages artificial intelligence to speed up incident resolution, automate repetitive tasks, and provide predictive insights into the health of your IT fleet.

> **Availability**: The AI Assistant is an **optional add-on** (+€30/month per user, flat rate) on top of your Hasfy Pro subscription. The Pro plan includes a quota of 3,000,000 AI tokens per month. See the [Pricing](/pricing) page for the full details.

## Assisted ticket resolution

### How it works

When a ticket is created, the AI assistant automatically analyzes:

1. **The title and description** of the ticket to understand the issue
2. **The history of similar tickets** resolved in your organization
3. **The metrics of the affected equipment** (if an agent is installed)
4. **The knowledge base** accumulated by Hasfy

Within seconds, the AI proposes one or more **resolution suggestions** directly in the ticket thread.

### Resolution suggestions

Suggestions appear in a dedicated panel at the top of the ticket, marked with the **"AI Suggestion"** badge. Each suggestion includes:

- **Probable diagnosis**: the AI identifies the most likely cause of the issue
- **Resolution steps**: a detailed, step-by-step procedure to resolve the incident
- **Confidence level**: an indicator of how reliable the suggestion is (high, medium, low)
- **Similar tickets**: links to past tickets that had the same issue

### Technician actions

When faced with an AI suggestion, the technician can:

- **Apply**: mark the suggestion as helpful and use it for resolution
- **Adapt**: modify the suggestion before applying it
- **Reject**: flag that the suggestion is not relevant (improves learning)
- **Ignore**: not use the suggestion without giving feedback

> **Important**: AI is a decision-support tool. The technician always remains the decision-maker and is responsible for the final resolution.

### Continuous improvement

The AI assistant continuously improves thanks to feedback from your team:

- **Applied** suggestions reinforce the models for similar cases
- **Rejected** suggestions adjust the weighting to avoid false positives
- The more your organization uses Hasfy, the more relevant and accurate the suggestions become

## Automatic categorization

### Classifying incoming tickets

When a ticket is created (particularly by an Employee user), the AI analyzes the content and automatically suggests:

- **Category**: Incident, Service request, Maintenance
- **Subcategory**: Hardware, Software, Network, Printing, Access, Other
- **Suggested priority**: based on the perceived urgency in the message and the potential impact
- **Automatic tags**: keywords extracted from the ticket content

These suggestions are pre-filled into the ticket fields. The technician or administrator can change them at any time.

### Duplicate detection

Before a ticket is created, the AI checks whether similar tickets are already open:

- If a **potential duplicate** is detected, the user is notified and can choose to attach themselves to the existing ticket rather than creating a new one
- If **similar resolved tickets** exist, a solution is proposed directly to the user

## Predictive analysis

### Predictive maintenance

By analyzing the metrics reported by agents over an extended period, the AI identifies **degradation trends**:

| Indicator | AI analysis | Recommended action |
|------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Disk gradually filling up | Predicted saturation in 15 days | Clean up or add disk space |
| Gradual increase in CPU usage | Possible hardware degradation or rogue process | Investigate processes |
| Frequency of restarts | Abnormal number of restarts detected | Check hardware (RAM, power supply) |
| Frequent agent disconnections | Network issue or system instability | Check connectivity |

Predictive maintenance alerts appear on the dashboard under the **"AI Recommendations"** section and can automatically generate preventive maintenance tickets.

### Ticketing trend analysis

The AI analyzes patterns in your tickets to identify:

- **Recurring spikes**: days or periods when ticket volume increases
- **Rising categories**: types of issues that are becoming increasingly frequent
- **Problematic equipment**: machines generating an abnormal number of tickets
- **Abnormal resolution times**: tickets taking longer than average to resolve

These insights are presented in a monthly report accessible from **Dashboard &gt; AI Insights**.

## Automatic responses

### First-level responses

For common requests (password reset, usage guides, FAQ), the AI can provide an immediate **automatic response** to the user:

1. The user creates a ticket
2. The AI identifies a common request with a high confidence level
3. A detailed response is automatically posted on the ticket
4. The user can indicate whether the response solved their problem
5. If the issue persists, the ticket is automatically escalated to a technician

> **Configuration**: Automatic responses are disabled by default. Enable them from **Settings &gt; AI &gt; Automatic responses** and set the minimum confidence threshold (recommended: 85%).

## AI configuration

### Available settings

Go to **Settings &gt; AI** to configure:

| Setting | Description | Default |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Resolution suggestions | Enable/disable suggestions on tickets | Enabled |
| Automatic categorization | Pre-fill category, priority, and tags | Enabled |
| Duplicate detection | Check for duplicates when a ticket is created | Enabled |
| Predictive maintenance | Analyze metric trends | Enabled |
| Automatic responses | Automatically respond to common requests | Disabled |
| Confidence threshold | Minimum level required to display a suggestion | 70% |

### Data privacy

Hasfy's AI processes data **strictly within your organization**:

- An organization's data is never used to train another organization's models
- Base models are pre-trained on anonymized IT-domain data
- Fine-tuning is performed exclusively on your organization's data
- You can disable all AI features at any time without losing any data
